Physical and chemical properties 

Tungsten is a hard brittle solid whose color ranges from steel-gray to nearly white. Its melting point is the highest of any metal, 3,410°C (6,170°F) and its boiling point is about 5,900°C (10,600°F). Tungsten's density is about 19.3 grams/cubic centimeter.  In addition, tungsten is a very conductive metal. Some chemical properties of tungsten are that it's a relatively inactive metal. It does not combine with oxygen at room temperatures (so no need to sumberge it in oil/lubricant). Tungsten corrodes(rust) at temperatures above 400°C (700°F). Tungsten does not react very readily with acids, although it does dissolve in nitric acid or aqua regia. Aqua regia is a mixture of hydrochloric and nitric acids.
