The ink

how pens use ink 

Well, in order to find out about the ink in a ballpoint pen, we must first find out about the pens themselves. How do they work? Well, to put it simply, a small, rolling ball is held in place by a socket, and a reservoir of ink coats the ball as it is used. Eventually, the modern ink we use today was discovered by Laszlo Biro, a hungarian newspaper editor. He noticed that the thick ink used on the newspapers dried instantly and didn’t smudge unlike thin fountain pen ink. He then took this formula, refined it and applied it to the ballpoint pen.

What is Inside the ink?
Now that we know where the modern ballpoint pen came from and how it works, what’s in it? Well, it has certain properties unique to it that gives it the identity of everyday ballpoint pen ink. For example, the manufacturer must create the perfect balance of viscosity, for it to be thick as possible while still being able to flow down freely. Once the perfect viscosity of the initial liquid is achieved, it is mixed with pigments or dye to create the color. The difference between pigments and dye is that while pigments are tiny, colored particles that are suspended in the ink solution, the dye is completely soluble in the liquid. For this reason, dye is most commonly used because the particles of the pigment can possibly clog the pen. The solvent of most inks is water or oil, with Oil being the most common because it is fast-drying and waterproof.

renew-ability 

Roughly 50% of ballpoint ink is made of the aforementioned dye. Different color inks have different color bases. For example, black ink uses carbon black, which is a fine powder made from burning hydrocarbons, red ink is made from eosin, etc. Unfortunately, the ink is made from non renewable resources. The inks contain many organic solvents, heavy metals, and oils that are not renewable. Without the pen, we wouldn’t be able to share information like we do today. Everyday tasks like note taking, mailing letters, calligraphy, art, would be impossible, so we should all make an effort not to waste pens. Many people lose pens because of their rather small figure, but what they don’t know is that it’s a small portion of the world’s ink supply lost permanently, never to be gained back.
